Sans-Serif

Sans-serif fonts are clean and modern, often used for digital content due to their readability on screens.

Examples of sans-serif fonts include Arial, Helvetica, and Verdana.

They lack the small decorative strokes (serifs) at the ends of letters.

Sans-serif fonts are commonly used in minimalist and contemporary designs.

Serif

Serif fonts have small decorative strokes or lines at the ends of their characters.

They are often associated with tradition, formality, and readability in print.

Examples of serif fonts include Times New Roman, Georgia, and Garamond.

Serif fonts are commonly used in books, newspapers, and formal documents.

Cursive

Cursive fonts mimic the style of handwritten script with flowing, connected letters.

They are often used for decorative purposes or to convey elegance and creativity.

Examples of cursive fonts include Brush Script, Pacifico, and Lobster.

Cursive fonts are less commonly used for body text due to their reduced readability.

Monospace

Monospace fonts have characters that occupy the same amount of horizontal space.

They are often used in coding, technical documents, and typewriters.

Examples of monospace fonts include Courier, Consolas, and Monaco.

Monospace fonts are valued for their alignment and clarity in structured text.
